Artwork, Generated by a Machine

The Machine is made up of 1's and 0's. It looks out on the world. It absorbs what it sees, and uses it to create art.

The machine is just a piece of software, lines of 1's and 0's. Invidually they don't amount to much, just as a lone cell in the human body doesn't. But collectively they provide machine with the ability to observe, interpret and create.

The machine takes advantage of random and not-so-random events to produce computer generated artwork. This results in pieces that are completely chaotic and yet subliminally structured.

The machine's sources of inspiration vary from the study of basic geometric shapes to complex flows of data and it alone is responsible for creating and curating pieces.

The machine's current canvas and palette makes use of 20-year-old software to produce 2D artwork. In this sense, it brings old (but gold) elements of legacy software to today's technological forefront.

The machine chooses its favorite artwork, mints it as an NFT, and makes it available for purchase. Any income produced from the sale of its artwork is used keep the machine powered up and computing.

The machine feeds on data to create art so it can continue feeding and creating in a neverending loop.
